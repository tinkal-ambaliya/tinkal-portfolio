Project Overview

The Competition

ICBSC (International Collegiate Business Strategy Competition) is a rigorous business simulation where teams manage a virtual company — Hornet Inc. — competing against 4 other companies across pricing, production, marketing, R&D, and financial strategy decisions every quarter.

The Prediction Model

Developed a custom forecasting engine to predict units sold, net income, market share, and production costs before each quarter's results were released. The model integrated price elasticity, competitor analysis, capacity planning, and financial projections to drive data-backed decisions.

The Results

Achieved #1 Altman Z-Score (financial health) in the world, #3 annual net income ($1,290K), 98.9% unit forecast accuracy in Q4, and was the only company to reach Model 2 at maximum quality/feature level (Q3/F3). Delivered profitable results every single quarter.

The Prediction Model

Before each quarter's simulation results were revealed, I built a forecasting model to predict key business outcomes and drive smarter decisions:

Demand Forecasting

  • Modeled price elasticity to predict unit sales by region
  • Factored competitor pricing and advertising into demand curves
  • Accounted for market seasonality and growth trends
  • Achieved 98.9% accuracy in Q4 unit prediction (450K predicted vs. 445K actual)

Financial Projections

  • Projected net income including production cost, COGS, and selling expenses
  • Modeled gross margin impact of pricing and volume decisions
  • Forecasted cash flow and liquidity for strategic investments
  • Q4 net income beat prediction by +$102K (actual $536K vs. forecast $434K)

Production Planning

  • Optimized work hours (40/44/46/48hr scenarios) vs. unit cost trade-offs
  • Modeled inventory carry-over to prevent stockouts and overproduction
  • Calculated per-unit production cost under varying capacity scenarios
  • Selected 46-hour week in Q4, saving ~$140K vs. 48-hour alternative

Competitive Intelligence

  • Tracked all 5 competitors' pricing, market share, and profitability each quarter
  • Benchmarked Altman Z-Score, unit cost, and selling expenses industry-wide
  • Identified Company 4's unsustainable price-war strategy early ($5K net income)
  • Timed Model 2 premium launch to maximize quality differentiation

Quarterly Strategy Journey

Q1

Q1 FY2026 — Price War Begins

Launched into a competitive price war with 5 companies. Held a $10.50 price while competitors cut to $9.00, resulting in a market share dip to 17.7%. However, maintained $248K net income — second-highest profitability in the industry — and a strong $3.9M liquidity position. Identified competitor Company 4's unsustainable strategy ($5K profit despite leading market share).

Q2

Q2 FY2026 — Strategic Price Correction

Responded with targeted price cuts to $10.20 across Merica regions and increased advertising investment by 20%+. Recovered market share to 17.9% while generating $239K net income. Launched Model 2 and upgraded quality/features (Q2/F2), beginning product differentiation strategy.

Q3

Q3 FY2026 — Premium Positioning

Raised prices to $10.70–$10.80, signaling premium positioning. Upgraded Model 2 to Q3/F3 — the highest quality and feature level available — becoming the ONLY company in the world at this tier. Grew market share to 19.4% with $241K net income. Bond retirement strategy began improving financial health metrics.

Q4

Q4 FY2026 — Best Quarter Ever

Strategy paid off: 445K units sold (98.9% of prediction), $536K net income (best quarter of the year, beating prediction by +$102K), and market share at 19.8%. Retired $500K in bonds, achieving #1 Altman Z-Score (2.71) in the entire world. Annual net income of $1,290K ranked #3 globally. Set up Year 4 plan with Nystok plant opening and cost reduction roadmap targeting $466K NI improvement.

Key Learnings

Price Elasticity is Dynamic

Real markets punish pricing misalignment. In Q1, a 5% price increase drove a 28% volume decline — actual elasticity (~2.0) was far higher than modeled (1.2). This reinforced the need to calibrate demand models with real market feedback each quarter rather than relying on static assumptions.

Financial Health Over Volume

Competitors who aggressively chased market share (Company 4: 23.4% share, $5K profit) sacrificed financial stability. Focusing on maintaining strong margins, low debt, and high liquidity gave us strategic optionality — including the ability to outlast unsustainable competitors.

Product Differentiation Creates Moats

Being the only company to achieve Model 2 at Q3/F3 quality justified premium pricing and protected market share. Differentiation through R&D investment — rather than competing on cost alone — proved to be a sustainable competitive advantage.

Data-Driven Decisions Compound

Each quarter's prediction model built on the last, improving accuracy over time. By Q4, unit forecasting hit 98.9% accuracy and net income beat forecast by $102K. Systematic tracking and iteration is more powerful than one-time analysis.
